Escaping the Trap of Excuses
“The lazy person is full of excuses, saying, ‘If I go outside, I might meet a lion in the street and be killed!’”
Proverbs 22:13 (NLT)
What is an excuse? It is the reason you give inorder to avoid doing something or being held responsible for certain actions.

In our opening scripture, God attaches excuses to a life of laziness. He says, “The lazy person is full of excuses, saying, ‘If I go outside, I might meet a lion in the street and be killed!’”

Excuses are well meaning reasons for justifying one’s reason for not acting as demanded. However, they are a trap because they make you lazy and don’t take away the consequences of not doing what is required of you.

Forexample, if you refuse to work hard, poverty will not excuse you; it will definitely attack you.

The cure for excuses is discipline and being responsible for your life. Discipline demands of you to act as you should in every situation regardless of how you feel. It is the ability to control your emotions and stick to the goals and plan you set for your life. Discipline is your ability to function by the set principles and not be carried away by the prevailing circumstances.

The truth is that no one wants to work hard everyday or wake up late in the night to pray and plan. However, all successful people will tell you that it is by doing those things you don’t want to do, that you get to your desired future. Success has a price and that price is disciplined action coupled with sacrifice. Excusing yourself from these two, automatically nullifies your potential for success.

Wisdom teaches you to value work and understand that it is a blessing and not a burden or ‘hustle’. God Himself said, “I will bless the works of your hands”. Jesus said “I must work the works of Him that sent me while it is day”. All the patriarchs including father Abraham were hard workers. Paul the great Apostle said, “I labored more abundantly than them all”.

So, as a child of God don’t give any excuse for not working. Be passionate and diligent in your work. Always give your very best in service to God and at your workplace. This is God’s wisdom for escaping the trap of excuses and its consequences which include poverty and the frustration that comes from unfulfilled goals. Glory to Jesus!
