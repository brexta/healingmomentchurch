Tuesday, July 7th, 2026 · Apostle Julius P. Isabirye
Leadership Series: In the Silent Years
“And the little boy grew and became strong in spirit; and he was in the deserts (wilderness) until the day of his appearing to Israel [the commencement of his public ministry].”
Luke 1:80 (AMP)
It takes time for God to make you a leader. There are several phases and processes He takes you through to make you the man or woman you are created to be in this world.

1 Timothy 3:6 warns us not to put novices or immature people in positions of ministry. The point is that the training of God is necessary to mature you and turn you into a great leader.

One of the major aspects of God’s leadership training process are the silent years. Silent years represent the years of your obscurity from the public eye. They are years when you are being formed by God in character and spiritual stature.

Our opening scripture gives us an example of John the Baptist. Though he was born with a leadership mandate to go before the Lord and prepare the way for Him, he experienced many silent years in the wilderness - growing strong in Spirit before his manifestation to Israel.

The silent years are years when God shapes the leader’s character, changes their thinking to align with His, and clearly reveals to them His purpose and assignment for their life. It is also the time when God anoints you, awakens the gifts of the Spirit in your life, and teaches you how to function in the anointing He has given you.

It is sometimes called the wilderness experience. This is because it is filled with several tests that come to mature you and turn you into a responsible leader. With every test, there is a lesson to learn and wisdom to imparted to your heart.

Jesus experienced 18 silent years before His manifestation. Moses also experienced 40 silent years before God appeared to him at the burning bush and called him to be deliverer of Israel. Bible scholars say that Joseph also spent 18 years of testing (in Potiphar’s house and in prison) before he became prime minister and walked in the reality of God’s dreams for his life.

As a leader, silent years don’t mean that God has forgotten you; He is simply working on you to perfect all that concerns you. He is with you and building you into the leader He ordained you to be. He is strengthening your spirit, imparting wisdom and making clear His vision for your life. So, don’t panic or be discouraged. Accept God’s training and believe that in due time your vision will speak. Hallelujah!
✨ Healing Insight
Understand that whatever you exalt, is what you worship. What you worship is what you truly believe in. Exalting God and His word above the contrary situations, is a sign of worship to Him. It pleases Him and provokes His power to work for you. This is the challenge of faith.
📖 Reading PlanJob 32–33 · Acts 14
🔖 MeditationNumbers 13:30–33
🙏 Prayer

Heavenly Father, I thank You for every promise You have made to me in Your word. I believe that Your Word is true and it will be fulfilled as You said. I reject every form of doubt and unbelief that trys to hinder me from walking in Your promises to me. I declare that I am becoming all that You promised me, in Jesus Mighty Name!
