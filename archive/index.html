Tuesday, June 9th, 2026Ephesians 2:6 (NKJV)
Seated With Christ
▼
“And raised us up together, and made us sit together in the heavenly places in Christ Jesus.”
Ephesians 2:6 (NKJV)
Christianity is a life of power and not weakness and defeat. We have been brought into the Kingdom of God through faith in Jesus Christ. The scripture teaches us that the kingdom of God is not in word but in power (1 Cor. 4:20).

This is very important for us to know as children of God. We are not powerless — we have all the power of God at our disposal. This power gives us dominion over sin, sickness, the world, the devil and demons.

Our opening scripture is one of those key verses that can open our eyes to how much power is available to us. It says that God has raised us up together and made us sit together in heavenly places in Christ Jesus. We are seated with Christ in the heavenly places.

But we know that Christ is seated at the right hand of the Father. The phrase ‘right hand’ means the place of authority and power. So, sitting with Christ in the heavenly places means that we have been lifted to a place of authority and great power in God.

As a child of God, you are seated with Christ at the right hand of authority and power. This means that you have all the ability you need to walk away from any sinful habit, live a life free from sickness and disease, and be able to achieve all your dreams. You can do all things through the power available to you in Christ.

There is no limit to how much you can become, how much liberty you can walk in, and how much you can accomplish. You are seated with Christ in a place of authority and power. So, choose to walk in this power and live the life that God has made available to you in Christ, in Jesus Name.
✨ Healing Insight
There is no limit to how much you can become, how much liberty you can walk in, and how much you can accomplish. You are seated with Christ in a place of authority and power.

Wednesday, June 10th, 2026Joshua 1:6 (AMP)
Building Confidence in Leadership
▼
“Be strong (confident) and of good courage, for you shall cause this people to inherit the land which I swore to their fathers to give them.”
Joshua 1:6 (AMP)
One of the most important demands of leadership is confidence. You cannot lead afraid or timid — you must lead confident.

Joshua had to learn this lesson immediately after taking over Moses’ position as leader of the children of Israel. God commanded him to be confident and courageous. It was key to his leadership effectiveness.

So, how do you build confidence to lead in business, at home, at the workplace, and in ministry?

The answer is in Joshua 1:8. God didn’t only command Joshua to be confident — He also showed him how. He said, “Keep this Book of the Law always on your lips; meditate on it day and night, so that you may be careful to do everything written in it. Then you will be prosperous and successful.” (NCV)

Genuine and lasting confidence in leadership is built on the discipline of hearing and understanding God’s Word. What God says to you in His Word is the material for being a confident leader in your company, at home and in ministry.

To every leader, God speaks specific words that are not general but personal. These words come from the pages of the Bible but are uniquely inspired to speak directly into the leader’s life and strengthen their heart in God.

As you hear from God, faith and confidence is built. The words God speaks to you erase all your fears and doubts. They are your weapons for confronting and defeating every opposing force on your leadership journey.

Leaders are raised by the Word of God. They are built and perfected through the knowledge of God’s Word. Their confidence is built on the Word of God — it is the sure foundation for victory and great success.

So, as a leader, it is very important for you to know the Word of God. It is your main platform for building confidence for effective leadership. Glory to Jesus!
✨ Healing Insight
Genuine and lasting confidence in leadership is built on the discipline of hearing and understanding God’s Word. What God says to you in His Word is the material for being a confident leader in your company, at home and in ministry.

Thursday, June 11th, 2026Romans 4:19-21 (KJV)
Consider The Word
▼
“And being not weak in faith, he considered not his own body now dead, when he was about an hundred years old, neither yet the deadness of Sarah’s womb: He staggered not at the promise of God through unbelief; but was strong in faith, giving glory to God; And being fully persuaded that, what he had promised, he was able also to perform.”
Romans 4:19-21 (KJV)
Every believer is involved in one fight — it is called the good fight of faith. The good fight of faith is the continual battle to walk in the promises of God. It is the fight to walk in all that Christ has made available for you through His finished work of redemption.

For example, Christ has made healing available, but you must possess it by faith. When Satan tries to put sickness on your body, you should use your faith in God’s Word to resist him and walk in divine health. Faith is your ability to resist the devil and his works. It is your ability to connect to all that the grace of God has made available through Christ.

One of the most fundamental parts of the good fight of faith is learning what to consider and what not to consider. In our opening scripture, Abraham considered not his own body (too old to father a child) and the deadness of Sarah’s womb.

The phrase ‘consider not’ doesn’t mean you don’t see it, but that you don’t pay close attention to it. It means to regard not. It is a fight to reject negative thoughts, opinions and feelings from controlling your life and moving you away from faith in God’s Word.

Though Abraham’s body was too old and his wife’s womb was dead, he refused to pay close attention to those facts and regard them as a reason to doubt God. Instead, he chose to focus on the promise God made to him. He considered the promise of God and by focusing on it, he was able to continue in faith until he obtained the miracle of Isaac.

In the fight of faith, what you choose to focus on and pay close attention to determines whether you win or fail. If Abraham had focused on his body and the condition of Sarah’s womb, he would have staggered at the promise of God through unbelief. His faith was only kept strong and became victorious because he focused on God and His sure promise to him.

So, learn to exalt God and His Word above what you see in your body, feel or hear around you. The Word of God should stay before your eyes at all times in spite of any negative medical report. As you pay close attention to God and His promise and refuse to consider the negative facts around you, God’s power will prevail and work miracles for you, in Jesus’ Name!
✨ Healing Insight
In the fight of faith, what you choose to focus on and pay close attention to determines whether you win or fail.

Friday, June 12th, 2026Romans 12:2 (NLT)
Let God Transform You
▼
“Don’t copy the behavior and customs of this world, but let God transform you into a new person by changing the way you think. Then you will know what God wants you to do, and you will know how good and pleasing and perfect his will really is.”
Romans 12:2 (NLT)
After receiving Jesus Christ as your Lord and Saviour, the next phase is being transformed.

Transformation is the process God takes you through to change you from being a baby Christian into a mature Christian. It is the process of changing from being a believer in Christ into becoming His disciple.

The ultimate goal of transformation is to change you into the image and likeness of Christ. It helps you become like Jesus in word, thought, purpose and action.

Our opening scripture teaches us that we are transformed by God through the changing of our thinking. Through the teaching of God’s Word, God reshapes your way of thinking so that you adopt the thinking pattern of Christ.

By changing the way you think, God helps you see things His way and start acting like Christ. This means that the greatest obstacle to changing into the image of Christ is an unrenewed mind.

Until your thinking pattern is changed by the Word of God, it is impossible to manifest the character and power of Christ in you. God changes your way of thinking so that your spirit man is not hindered in expression.

Joshua 1:8 and Psalm 1:2 show us that this transformation takes place as you give yourself continually to studying, meditating on, confessing and obeying the Word of God.

Meditation stands out among all because it is the process through which the seed of God’s Word is directly planted in your mind. By thinking on God’s Word daily, you start adopting the thoughts, ways and mindset of heaven. This helps you start acting like a child of God and walk in His perfect will for your life.

Let God transform you because only the transformed Christians find their purpose and easily fulfil it. Let God transform you because it is the key to becoming a disciple of Christ. Let God transform you because it is the key to manifesting both the character and power of Christ in this world. Hallelujah!
✨ Healing Insight
The ultimate goal of transformation is to change you into the image and likeness of Christ. It helps you become like Jesus in word, thought, purpose and action.

Saturday, June 13th, 20261 Peter 2:24 (GNT)
Your Inheritance in Christ
▼
“Christ himself carried our sins in his body to the cross, so that we might die to sin and live for righteousness. It is by his wounds that you have been healed.”
1 Peter 2:24 (GNT)
Faith is a result of having a clear knowledge of God’s Word. Until the Word of God is made clear to you, it is difficult to have faith.

One of the main purposes of God’s Word is to reveal to us our inheritance in Christ. An inheritance refers to that which is rightfully yours by law — your appointed possession because of the unique relationship you have with someone.

When Christ died for us, He not only redeemed us but also purchased for us an inheritance in God. In Psalm 103:2, David prophetically called this inheritance ‘benefits’. He said, “Bless the Lord O my soul and forget none of His benefits.” He helps us understand that there are many benefits for being connected to Jesus Christ as your Lord and Saviour. These benefits are our inheritance — our rightful legal possession through faith in Jesus Christ.

Verse 3 says, “Who forgives all your sins and heals all your diseases.” These two are our first and foremost redemptive benefits. All our sins are forgiven and all our diseases are healed. This is the same message echoed in 1 Peter 2:24. Christ died for us to obtain righteousness, and by His wounds we were healed of all sickness and disease.

As a believer in Christ, forgiveness of sins and divine healing are your lawful inheritance. They are rightfully yours because of your connection to Christ as your Lord and Saviour. This means that God is always ready to forgive all your sins and heal your body of every disease because of your relationship with Christ.

The death of Christ is the foundation you should always stand upon and be assured of forgiveness for all your sins and healing for all sickness.

By the death of Jesus, the court of heaven already ruled that you are an heir of righteousness and divine healing. So all you have to do is claim your inheritance through faith and you will have it, in Jesus’ Name. That is the knowledge that makes faith for forgiveness of sins and divine healing simple. It is your inheritance, in Jesus’ Name!

Sunday, June 14th, 2026John 14:19 (GNT)
Because Jesus Lives: Hope Beyond Physical Death
▼
“In a little while the world will see me no more, but you will see me; and because I live, you also will live.”
John 14:19 (GNT)
One of the greatest fears of mankind is the fear of death. Many people will pay any price to stay alive, yet with all their efforts, they eventually die. Even science has tried to find a solution but it has failed.

The good news is that one of the main reasons why Jesus came was to deliver us from the fear of death. Hebrews 2:14 says He defeated the devil for us so that we can be free from the fear of death.

Paul writes and asks, “O death, where is your victory? O death, where is your sting?” For sin is the sting that results in death, and the law gives sin its power. How we thank God, who gives us victory over sin and death through Jesus Christ our Lord! (1 Corinthians 15:55-57 NLT).

The sting of death is sin and the strength of death is the law, but Jesus has set us free from both of them. Through His blood all our sins were washed away and forgiven. Through His sacrifice on the Cross, He delivered us from the curse of the law. We are not under the law but under grace and sin no longer has dominion over us (Gal. 3:13-14, Romans 6:1-14).

The purpose of the resurrection of Christ was to give us hope beyond physical death. There is hope of being alive after death. As children of God, our hearts should never be troubled when a believer dies, for we know that they only transitioned and went to heaven to be with the Lord. Jesus said “I am the resurrection and the life. He who believes in Me, though he may die, he shall live” (John 11:25).

There is a solution to death and it is found in Jesus Christ. Because He defeated death and rose again, all those who put their faith in Him enjoy victory over sin and death. Salvation is the key to setting people free from sin and the fear of death. This is the gospel - the good news of Jesus Christ! Hallelujah!

Building a Strong Christian Life
▼
“This Book of the Law shall not depart from your mouth, but you shall meditate in it day and night, that you may observe to do according to all that is written in it. For then you will make your way prosperous, and then you will have good success.”
Joshua 1:8 (NKJV)
After receiving Jesus Christ as our Lord and Savior, the next part is pursuing growth. The purpose of growth is to make us strong in the Lord and in the power of His might. This positions us in a way so that we can withstand the evil attacks of the enemy. It also helps us rise to the place of discovering our God given purpose and be empowered to fulfill it.

So, how do we build a strong Christian life?

Our opening scripture gives us the way. The primary way is through confession of God’s word. By keeping God’s word in your mouth, you build a strong spiritual life and easily defeat the devil and his wicked plans.

Confession means speaking in agreement with God’s word. It means being in agreement with God in speech. It is filling your mouth with the word of God and making it your language.

Death and life are in the power of the tongue (Proverbs 18:21). Until your tongue is touched by God, your life can’t be changed. Confession is the pathway to allowing God to influence your life by influencing your tongue. It is the way you fill your words with life and not death.

What other people speak about you doesn’t affect you as much as what you speak about yourself does. Your words rule you more than any other person’s words. So, the easiest way to change your life is by changing what you say about yourself. And the best way to change your words, is by keeping God’s word in your mouth. Confessing God’s word, is the primary wisdom for building a strong Christian life. Glory to Jesus!

Tuesday, June 16th, 2026Joshua 1:8 (NKJV)
Building a Strong Christian Life (Part II)
▼
“This Book of the Law shall not depart from your mouth, but you shall meditate in it day and night, that you may observe to do according to all that is written in it. For then you will make your way prosperous, and then you will have good success.”
Joshua 1:8 (NKJV)
Building a strong Christian life means deliberately growing in Christ daily until you become what He purposed for your life. It is the process of doing those things demanded of you everyday inorder to rise to your appointed place of destiny in Christ.

1 Peter 2:2 reveals to us that at the onset of our salvation, we are “new born babies” and we need to desire the sincere milk of the word so that we can grow up. So, no one starts out in Christ as a mature Christian; we all need to grow up unto maturity.

It is also important to note that there is a great difference between being a Christian and being a strong Christian. Being a Christian is by receiving Jesus as your Lord and Savior but it is through daily effort that you become a strong Christian.

In yesterday’s devotion, I taught on confessing God’s word as the primary way to build a strong Christian life. The second way to build a strong Christian life is by continually meditating on the word of God.

Meditation refers to filling your mind with God’s word daily. It is the act of deliberately separating yourself from all things that distract, to think on the scriptures.

Through meditation on the word, you replace old thoughts with new ones; ungodly thinking patterns are replaced with godly ones. The impure is replaced with the pure; what comes from the devil is deleted and replaced with that which is from God.

Meditation allows the word of God to sink down into your heart. It is the process of “chewing the word of God until it is effectively digested”. The word in your mind is information, but as you meditate on it, it becomes a revelation in your heart. It is that revelation that changes your thoughts and transforms your character eternally.

So, it is wisdom to practice the habit of giving yourself wholly to the word of God through deliberate sessions of meditation. 1 Timothy 4:15, Joshua 1:8, and Psalm 1:2-3 assure you that as you meditate on God’s word, you will become a great success. Meditation on God’s word, is a sure platform for building you into a strong and very successful christian so that your life glorifies God in this world in all things. Glory to Jesus!

Wednesday, June 17th, 2026Joshua 1:8 (NKJV)
Building a Strong Christian Life (Part III)
▼
“This Book of the Law shall not depart from your mouth, but you shall meditate in it day and night, that you may observe to do according to all that is written in it. For then you will make your way prosperous, and then you will have good success.”
Joshua 1:8 (NKJV)
A strong Christian is one who can stand the tests and trials in this world. He is one with clear knowledge about His identity in Christ and the power of God working for him. The strong Christian is aware of God’s purpose for their life and is empowered to pursue it.

In the previous devotions, we discussed two ways to build a strong Christian life; confession and meditation on God’s word.

The third way to build a strong Christian life is by practicing the word of God. Practicing the word of God involves acting on the word of God. It refers to walking in obedience to the principles of scripture.

Matthew 7:24-25 (NIV) reveals this truth saying, “Therefore everyone who hears these words of mine and puts them into practice is like a wise man who built his house on the rock. The rain came down, the streams rose, and the winds blew and beat against that house; yet it did not fall, because it had its foundation on the rock.”

A strong Christian life is built on daily practice of God’s word. As you hear and act on the scriptures, you rise to higher levels of spiritual strength. This enables you to stand strong in times of testing without falling.

In the natural, eating food nourishes your body, but going to the gym, and engaging in work, are the ones that make your body strong. If you keep feeding on the word but don’t practice it, it won’t make you strong. The word that you practice is the one that builds you and makes you strong enough to withstand all opposing forces.

James 1:22 (GNT) says, “Do not deceive yourselves by just listening to his word; instead, put it into practice.” This verse teaches us that if we only hear the word and do not obey what is says, we deceive ourselves. Practicing the word is what connects you to the spiritual strength God has for you.

So, choose to be a doer of the word of God and not just a hearer. Let the word of God be the foundation for all your decisions and actions at your workplace, in your home and in your ministry. This is the key to building a strong Christian life, in Jesus Name.

▼

1 Corinthians 6:13 (T4T)

We worship Him in spirit and in truth, but we can also worship Him with our bodies.

God’s desire is that He be Lord over your entire being - spirit, soul and body. He wants to express Himself through you in an unlimited way.

So, everytime you are called upon to participate in any activity that promotes the spread of God’s kingdom, it is your opportunity to present your body as an instrument for serving God. As you offer your skills, energy and talents to serve the purposes of God, you are telling God that He is Lord over your body. That service cannot be ignored by God; it is an act of worship to Him. Saturday, June 20th, 20261 Corinthians 6:13 (HCSB)
The Lord for the Body
▼
“Food for the stomach and the stomach for food,” but God will do away with both of them. The body is not for sexual immorality but for the Lord, and the Lord for the body.
1 Corinthians 6:13 (HCSB)
In Yesterday’s devotion, we focused on using our bodies to serve the Lord. I explained that serving God with your body is an act of worship. Today, I want to focus your attention on the phrase “... and the Lord for the body” as used in our opening scripture.

What does it mean for the Lord to be for your body?

The phrase ‘the Lord for the body,’ simply means that God is not against your body. He is not an enemy to your body. As simple as this sounds, it reveals something very deep and important.

Everytime your body is attacked by disease and pain, never think that it is from God. No pain or disease in your body ever comes from God because God is for your body and not against it.

Disease and pain are against your bodily health. They therefore do not come from God but from the enemy.

Satan is the cause of sickness and pain because he is the one against your body. He is not for your body. He seeks to steal your health and kill your body (John 10:10).

When you understand this truth, you will not treat any pain or disease as normal. It will build within you a zeal and faith to successfully resist every kind of pain Satan uses to attack your body.

Focus means being where you are per time and doing what is required of you with all your heart and strength. It is the ability to totally concentrate and do what is required of you until it is accomplished.

Focus is key to effectiveness. Whatever you do with maximum concentration, always yields the greatest results. Focus helps you maximize your strength and skills in the pursuit of the right goals.

Distractions are Satan’s tools for reducing your effectiveness and making you a mediocre person. So, learn to focus on each task at hand. This is your wisdom for becoming an effective and excellent worker, in Jesus Name.
